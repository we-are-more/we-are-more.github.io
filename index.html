Empowering change: Mindful language and content choices against misogyny

Dear Young Adults,

Are you passionate about creating a world where everyone is treated with respect and equality? Do you believe in the power of your voice to enact positive change? Join us for an immersive workshop that delves into the critical issue of sexist attitudes and empowers you to be special agents of unmasking media and of transformation in our society.

In a world where sexism still lingers, our workshop, "Empowering Change: Mindful Language and Content Choices Against Misogyny" aims to ignite a fire within you to challenge the status quo and foster a more inclusive society.

Why Attend?

Our workshop doesn't just scratch the surface – it dives deep into the influence of language and media on perpetuating sexist attitudes. We'll explore how seemingly harmless words and content choices can reinforce harmful stereotypes and contribute to a culture of inequality.

Our research has peeled back the layers of media influence and discovered something pretty alarming: women are often treated like products, just tools for big influencers and brands to make a profit. It's frustrating to see our complex minds reduced to simple marketing tactics.

Media content bombards us with images that just don't ring true. Instead of showing the real power and diversity of women, it dishes out stereotypes and uses hurtful language to keep them in place.

From overly sexualized stereotypes to ads selling happiness in a bottle, it can feel like wading through a sea of clichés. And when it comes to social media, the echo chambers and extremes can leave us feeling more confused than enlightened.

Armed with this knowledge, we have the power to make a difference. Imagine sharing content that celebrates diversity and challenges stereotypes, or supporting creators who are paving the way for a more inclusive media landscape.

But hey, that's where our workshop comes in. It's not just about talking – it's about doing. We're creating a space where we can call out these issues, spread the word, and get people talking.

But we're not stopping there. We're turning our thoughts into art. Through creativity, we're showing the world that we're more than just what the media says we are. And what better place to do it than on social media?

So, come join us in this fight for change. Let's use our creativity and passion to show everyone that we're more than just stereotypes. Post your art, share your stories, and let's rewrite the script together.

What to Expect:

  • Critical Conversations: Engage in thought-provoking discussions that unpack the nuances of sexist language and its impact on societal attitudes.
  • Media Analysis: Explore the powerful influence of media on shaping perceptions of gender roles and identities, and learn to critically evaluate and deconstruct media messages.
  • Mindful Communication: Discover the transformative power of conscious communication and learn practical strategies for challenging sexist language and promoting inclusivity in your interactions.
  • Artistic Expression: Participate in an empowering art session where you'll create pieces to share on Instagram using #wearemore. Show the world that women are more than sexual objects – they are talented, capable, and multifaceted individuals with diverse strengths and skills.

Hey, we got you. Our team did research on the impact of exposure to misogynistic media content and its role in shaping gender stereotypes and sexist attitudes in young adult males aged 18-25, we sought insight into how media exposure actually influences the attitudes and behaviors of males. We conducted 12 interviews with males your age and found out some shocking but also promising results!

Factors influencing male perceptions 🚹 🧔

Our interviewees noted that mass media is full of misogynistic content that influences the way they view the world. Do you see a lot of content like this on your feed? One of our participants mentioned that it is impossible to not be influenced if this is the thing he sees.

“It's kind of hard not to be influenced when you're constantly exposed to certain images and messages, so I think probably everyone is, um, uh, everyone is influenced.” (I.K.)

They showed what is actually important for them as they demonstrated their preference in forming their own attitudes separate from ones pushed by the media.

Unfair treatment 👎 ⚖

Our participants also noticed that women often face unfair treatment in society, and try to overcome it in different ways.

“Some arguments, like when you fight like verbally with a girl, we'd be like, okay, why are you talking, you should be in the kitchen” (A.K.)

Do you feel like women belong in the kitchen? Of course not… Let's make a change and show men that do not see how strong women really are. Being able to fight with misogyny is not only for women, men go fight for your moms, sisters, girlfriends, and friends.

Women as object of sexualization 👠 👗

Did you know that our participants noticed that there is still a common trend in which women are portrayed in overly sexualized ways across various media forms like movies, TV, games, and music. They mentioned that in games like Grand Theft Auto (GTA) which you are probably familiar with, women are shown as a sexual desire portrayed with perfect bodies and almost nude.

“The women in the game [GTA] have perfect bodies from a men's perspective, and they are half naked” (I.K.)

Possible strategies for the change 🚀 👊

Do you want to learn about different solutions on how to fight with sexist representation of women? Answers of our participants could help you with that. They gave us potential strategies on how the negative effects of mass media can be avoided. They suggested giving age limits on internet activities and reducing the amount of nudity and backing the idea of keeping inappropriate content away from the younger generation.

“So I would change or make some kind of an age limit on those kinds of internet activities, let's say. So it wouldn't be as critical for young people” (T.R.)

Showing more women of achievement is also a great solution for the ongoing problem.

“I would like to see more women of with those kind of jobs I've mentioned telling their stories, um, and like actually showing what they do, etc.” (A.K.)
